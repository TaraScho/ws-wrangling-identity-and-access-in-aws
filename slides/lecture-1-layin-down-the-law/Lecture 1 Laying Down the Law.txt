Part 1: Layin’ down the law

What is 
IAM?
AWS IAM (Identity and Access Management) controls who can do what to which resources.
- Knows who you are (authentication)
- Knows what you're allowed to do (authorization)
- Enforces different rules for access (fine-grained access)

Why this matters
In the cloud identity is the perimeter
Long-lived cloud credentials are the are the most common cause of publicly documented cloud security breaches

Crafting IAM policies with PARC
Principal
The entity or WHO is allowed/denied
Actions
The verbs allowed or denied access
Resources
Cloud resources the actions apply to
Condition
Optional constraints that further refine when or where the policy is in effect.
{
  "Statement": [
    {
      "Principal": {
        "AWS": "arn:aws:iam::123456789012:role/sre-role"
      },
      "Action": "lambda:InvokeFunction",
      "Resource": "arn:aws:lambda:us-east-1:123456789012:function:test_lambda",
      "Condition": {
        "IpAddress": {
          "aws:SourceIp": "10.0.0.0/8"
        }
      }
    }
  ]
}

"Action": “ec2:Describe*”

Wildcards match multiple actions
